About

Governance clarity for boards that want to actually serve students

School Board Answers publishes practical, outcomes-focused Q&As for school board members, superintendents, and anyone who cares about whether public education governance is working the way it should.

Every question answered here comes from real situations: boards struggling to stay out of management decisions, boards with too many goals and no clear priorities, boards that haven't connected their time and money to actual student outcomes. The answers apply the principles of outcomes-focused governance — clear goals, honest monitoring, and a clean governance-management boundary.

New Q&As are published every two to three days. Subscribe to get them by email.
